## The problem through my Maslow tiers (2026-07-11, verbatim)

1. At the lowest level, I think the problem I have about health is good food. Being able to have a balanced diet that cover need for protien and vitamins. Seems like I have deficiencies. At personal level this sort of has an information problem more than an access/affordability problem. Food on my table has to change a bit. Also considering that I would also want my food to be sustainably sourced, cruelty free and possibly organic at times that does limit options like milk creating a limitation on sources of nutrients.
2. At a safety & security level, I feel having a sense of comfort with a medical facility is what is lacking. Consumerism in medical field has made consulting GPs and especially specialists tougher. There is lack of trust. And then also the entire process of finding a doctor, waiting for long durations for a 2 min consultation etc. are small things which compound into a sense of not looking for help. Access to trustworthy medical facilities and a comfort zone is missing. Sense of a family doctor.
3. In terms of love and relationship, it is about people caring for your health. That brings questions like are you eating healthy, are you exercising enough, do you have health insurance, did you consult a doctor for this to fore. When you have people you love then you start caring a little more about your own health too.
4. I think what we eat and how much we exercise have become public conversations. Being able to invest in healthy eating or exercising slowly contributes to the idea of self esteem that I am working on me and I am good at it. Often becoming sort of a primary or secondary identity. While in some cases it is gym, cooking my own food has been the thing in my case.
5. I feel longevity is the self actualisation problem when it comes to health. For me, it is actually a slow realisation but my body does feel very old inside while outside I keep claiming I am forever 27. Being able to extend your life and hence do more with your life. In fact, every frontier problem related to health we have solved ultimately has led to increased lifespan for human beings. Which might involve solving for currently most deadly diseases, access to healthcare, supplements to counter common dietary issues etc.

## Cross-tier insights

- **Loop, not ladder.** Every solved health frontier ultimately extended lifespan — tier 5 is what falls out of solving tiers 1–4, not a tier you aim at. The self-actualization question is answered by executing the physiological one.
- **The shared hole (tiers 1 + 2):** no consumer-side validation layer between commercial health claims and the person deciding — pharma distorts medicine, brand narratives distort food, and in both cases the seller's side owns all existing "validation."
- **The binding constraint:** any validation must cost ~zero attention to beat narrative (health is important-but-never-urgent). The known-working form is embodied trust — the family doctor, the known farmer.
- **The instrument exists but is disconnected.** Full-body checkups (growing 22–28%/yr) gave the middle class its deficiency data — but the panel lives in the distrusted medical system and never talks to the kitchen. The deficiency problem is national (anemia: 57% of women, 67% of under-5s, NFHS-5, rising); the visibility of it is class-skewed.
